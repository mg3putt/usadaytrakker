The rules explained

Two limits. Both matter.

182

USCBP Rolling Rule

US Customs and Border Protection limits Canadian visitors to 182 days in any rolling 365-day period. Exceed this and you risk being denied entry or losing your visitor status.

182

IRS Calendar Year Rule

The IRS tracks days per calendar year as part of the Substantial Presence Test. The weighted 3-year formula can make you a US tax resident even if you stayed under 182 days in a single year.

1 + ⅓ + ⅙

The SPT Formula

All days this year, plus ⅓ of last year's days, plus ⅙ of the year before. If this total reaches 183, you may be considered a US tax resident. USA Day Trakker calculates this for you automatically.

Form 8840

Closer Connection Exception

If you exceed the SPT threshold but maintain closer ties to Canada, you can file Form 8840 to claim the Closer Connection Exception. The app tracks the data you need to file it.
